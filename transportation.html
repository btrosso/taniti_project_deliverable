Transportation

Almost all visitors arrive to Taniti by air, landing at the island's small but efficient airport. This airport currently accommodates small jets and propeller planes, providing frequent connections to nearby major cities. Efforts to expand the airport are underway, aiming to allow larger jets to land and thereby increase the island's capacity for international travelers. This expansion is expected to significantly boost tourism and the local economy. For those who prefer to travel by sea, a small cruise ship docks in Yellow Leaf Bay for one night per week, allowing passengers to enjoy a brief but immersive experience of Taniti's charm. These cruise visits add a unique flavor to the island's tourism profile, offering a blend of maritime adventure and cultural exploration.

Once on the island, getting around Taniti is straightforward and convenient. Public buses serve Taniti City from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m. daily, making it easy to navigate the city's attractions and amenities without the need for a car. The bus system is reliable and affordable, catering to both locals and tourists, and offering a great way to mingle with residents and learn about the local culture. For those venturing beyond the city limits, private buses cover the rest of the island, ensuring that all areas are well-connected. These private buses often offer guided tours, providing insights into the island’s history and natural wonders. Taxis are readily available in Taniti City, providing a comfortable option for those who prefer private transportation. The taxi drivers are knowledgeable and often provide insightful tips about the best places to visit, making them more than just a means of transportation. Additionally, rental cars can be secured from a local agency near the airport, offering flexibility for exploring at one's own pace and discovering hidden gems around the island. Car rental agencies also provide detailed maps and travel guides, helping visitors plan their itineraries efficiently.

For the more adventurous or eco-conscious traveler, bicycles and helmets are available for rent from several vendors, with helmets being mandatory by law to ensure safety. This option is popular among visitors who enjoy a more active way of seeing the sights and reducing their carbon footprint. Taniti City is relatively flat and very walkable, making it a pleasure to explore on foot, with picturesque streets and friendly locals enhancing the experience. The pedestrian-friendly nature of the city encourages a leisurely pace, allowing visitors to fully appreciate the local culture and scenery. Many tourists choose to stay in the bustling area surrounding Merriton Landing, where they can easily access a variety of attractions, shops, and restaurants without the need for a vehicle. Merriton Landing’s vibrant atmosphere, with its mix of modern amenities and traditional charm, makes it a favorite spot for both new and returning visitors. This pedestrian-friendly approach not only enhances the visitor experience but also supports the island's commitment to sustainable tourism, reducing the carbon footprint and preserving Taniti's natural beauty for future generations. Additionally, many local businesses offer discounts to those who choose eco-friendly transportation methods, further encouraging sustainable travel habits.
